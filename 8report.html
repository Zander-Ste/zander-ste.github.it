RESERVES COURT OF CALIFORNIA

CIVICSIGHT GUIDELINE COMMITTEE

ADJUDICATOR SAMSON KINGS

CASE NO. 2043-CC-12345

DISCLOSURE OF CIVICSIGHT RESIDENTIAL CAMERAS

TRANSCRIPT OF PROCEEDINGS

HELD AT LOS ANGELES, CALIFORNIA

DATE: 15 JULY 2043

1 ADJUDICATOR KINGS: Good morning, everyone. Please be seated. We are here today to discuss the CivicSight residential surveillance program and the need for disclosure. Ms. Sharpe, could you please introduce the relevant context for those in the room who might be unfamiliar?
2 MS. SHARPE: Thank you Mr. Kings. Several days ago, an altercation occured at a house party in which one party was greviously injured, and remains hospitalised. The assailent was not initally aware of CivicSight cameras inside the house, and when he was informed of their presence and the subsequent recording of his crimes, he took his own life in a panic.
3 ADJUDICATOR KINGS: Thank you Ms. Sharpe. Now, Mr. Harris, would you please lead with CivicSight's policy so far?
4 MR. HARRIS: Currently, CivicSight recommends disclosure, but does not require it for participants in the program.
5 ADJUDICATOR KINGS: Does CivicSight state a reason for not enforcing disclosure, Mr. Harris?
6 MR. HARRIS: Yes. Many people who choose to opt in to the program do so because they are experiencing forms of domestic abuse, which might not be identified should the location of CivicSight cameras be disclosed.
7 ADJUDICATOR KINGS: Thank you Mr. Harris. Ms. Spencer, as the representitive for public wellbeing, do you have any questions you would like to ask Mr. Harris?
8 MS. SPENCER: None at this time.
9 ADJUDICATOR KINGS: Very well. Thank you all for the introduction. We will adjourn for a 5 minute break, and return to discuss the benefits of requiered disclosure, and how CivicSight would enforce such policy.
